Why Rook exists

Built to make local AI feel usable.

Rook is designed for people who want a cleaner way to work with models on their own machine. Instead of bouncing between terminals, notes, and disconnected chats, Rook gives you one focused interface for project tracking, saved memory, session history, image attachments, text context, and direct model testing.

Before installing

System requirements

Required

  • Windows computer
  • Ollama installed and running
  • At least one compatible Ollama model

Recommended

  • Modern multi-core CPU
  • 16 GB RAM or more
  • NVIDIA GPU for stronger local AI performance
  • 12 GB+ VRAM preferred for larger models

Good to know

  • Rook is the interface, not the AI model itself
  • Performance depends on your hardware and model choice
  • Private work is best kept on local models

Questions

FAQ

Does Rook include AI models?

No. Rook connects to models you install through Ollama, then gives you a cleaner desktop interface for using them.

Does Rook support cloud models?

Yes. Rook supports local Ollama models and supported cloud-connected Ollama models, so you can choose the right fit for the task.

Do users need to install Ollama first?

Not necessarily. Users can download Rook first. If Ollama is not already installed, the Rook installer opens the Ollama download page during setup to help get them started.

What does “choose your price” mean?

The V1 release is meant to work with a pay-what-you-want style payment flow, with a low entry point and the option for supporters to pay more.

Is this finished commercial software?

V1 should be presented honestly as an early public release or beta: usable, polished, and real, but still early in the product’s life.
